ROUND: 16

## Problem Frame
Sol Vida lacks a cohesive visual identity that clearly communicates its brand ethos of natural vitality and scientific efficacy, leading to a diluted market presence and an unclear value proposition in a competitive supplement space.

## Evidence / Receipts
- Existing brand assets show inconsistent application of color and typography.
- Market research indicates competitor brands are effectively leveraging clear, integrated design systems.
- User feedback suggests confusion about product positioning and benefits.

## Opportunity Areas
1. Develop a distinctive packaging aesthetic that harmonizes natural motifs with a sense of scientific precision.
2. Establish a clear visual hierarchy on packaging to guide consumer attention to key product information and brand messaging.
3. Optimize material choices for packaging to enhance perceived quality and align with sustainability values.

## Constraints
- Avoid generic wellness wallpaper aesthetics.
- Prioritize clarity, hierarchy, and material truth.

## Profile Context
- Candidate profiles: logo, typography, packaging, website
- Recommended profile for this round: packaging
- Packaging is the primary touchpoint where consumers interact with the product and make purchase decisions.
- A strong packaging design can immediately convey brand values and product efficacy, differentiating Sol Vida on the shelf.

## Loop Context
- Minimum reviews before ship: 3, Target acceptance review: 3, Maximum reviews: 4.

## Handoff to Strategy
- Refine the core brand narrative to emphasize the unique blend of natural and scientific elements.
- Identify specific visual elements (colors, textures, imagery) that best represent this dual ethos.
- Propose a clear messaging framework for packaging that highlights key benefits and differentiates Sol Vida.
