## Creative Thesis
To establish Sol Vida as a beacon of natural efficacy and transparency through packaging that clearly communicates benefits, prioritizes ingredient information, and leverages tactile and visual cues to convey authenticity and quality.

## Non-Negotiables
1. Product benefits must be immediately discernible on the front panel.
2. Key ingredients and their origin must be clearly communicated.
3. Brand identity must be consistent with natural and premium wellness.
4. Information hierarchy must guide the consumer effortlessly.
5. Packaging must convey a sense of material truth and quality.

## Visual System Rules
- Use a natural, earthy color palette with subtle accents for vibrancy.
- Employ clean typography with clear legibility for all information.
- Incorporate textures and finishes that suggest natural materials (e.g., matte, subtle grain).

## Copy Direction Rules
- Use concise, benefit-driven language.
- Emphasize natural ingredients and their specific functions.
- Maintain an authentic, trustworthy, and informative tone.

## Profile Enforcement
- Required deliverables: A clear visual system for packaging that ensures immediate clarity of product benefits and usage. An established clear hierarchy for information on the packaging, prioritizing key selling points and ingredients. Exploration of textures and finishes that communicate the authenticity and quality of natural ingredients.
- QA gates: All design decisions must be directly applicable to packaging, such as label layout, material choices, and visual cues for product information hierarchy.
- Profile constraints: Design execution must focus on achieving clarity, hierarchy, and material truth specifically on packaging for Sol Vida.

## Director Acceptance Timing
- Minimum reviews before ship: 3
- Target acceptance at: 3 reviews
- Hard maximum reviews: 4

## Handoff to Review
- Is the information hierarchy clear and effective?
- Does the design communicate natural efficacy and transparency?
- Are the proposed textures and finishes appropriate for the brand?
