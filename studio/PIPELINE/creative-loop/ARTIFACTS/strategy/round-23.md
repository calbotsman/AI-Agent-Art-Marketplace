## Audience
- Health-conscious consumers seeking authentic, trustworthy wellness products.
- Individuals who value transparency and ingredient efficacy over generic branding.

## Positioning
Sol Vida positions itself as the purveyor of honest, effective wellness, differentiated by a commitment to material truth and a clear, hierarchical design that communicates inherent purity.

## Message Pillars
1. Purity & Efficacy: Emphasizing the natural origin and scientifically backed benefits of ingredients.
2. Transparency & Trust: Communicating clear information hierarchy and honest design.
3. Elevated Simplicity: A premium yet approachable aesthetic that avoids wellness clichés.

## Proof Points
- Design will directly reflect material truth, avoiding artificiality.
- Information hierarchy will make key benefits and ingredients scannable.
- Visual language will be inspired by nature without being literal or generic.

## Strategic Risks
- Packaging could inadvertently fall into generic "wellness wallpaper" aesthetics.
- Design might fail to convey the "material truth" of the ingredients effectively.

## Designer Profile Selection
- Why this profile is correct for this round.
    - Packaging is the primary touchpoint for communicating product value and brand identity in the competitive supplement market.
    - It allows for direct application of material truth and hierarchy principles through physical form, print, and tactile elements.
- What constraints this profile imposes on execution.
    - The `packaging` profile will impose constraints on structural design, material finishes, and information layout to ensure brand identity and product value are clearly communicated within the physical product.

## Handoff to Creative Direction
- Define the core "material truth" Sol Vida's packaging should embody for this round: Authentic, unadulterated ingredient quality expressed through tactile finishes and minimalist forms.
