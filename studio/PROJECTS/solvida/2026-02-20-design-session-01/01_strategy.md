# Strategy Brief (Rowan)

## Positioning
Sol Vida should read as grounded energy: modern, measured, and materially credible, not optimistic wellness wallpaper.

## Audience
Primary: urban professionals and creatives (25-42) who value clean signals, practical health cues, and elevated packaging.
Secondary: trend-aware shoppers who reward coherent systems over loud novelty.

## Competitors + whitespace
- Crowded lane: pastel-soft wellness brands using vague positivity and undifferentiated serif+sans templates.
- Whitespace: disciplined, proof-forward wellness identity where clarity and hierarchy do most of the persuasion.

## Messaging pillars
1. Calm energy (sustained, not hype).
2. Credibility through specifics (ingredients, sugar, functional benefit).
3. Designed consistency across touchpoints (series over one-off hero artifacts).

## Proof / credibility
- Explicit hierarchy: flavor first, benefit second, ingredient support third.
- Repeatable system: one direction expressed across six surfaces.
- Clear claims adjacent to evidence where possible.

## Voice + keywords
Voice: direct, refined, warm but unsentimental.
Keywords: calm energy, low sugar, clarity, balance, functional, modern.

## Risks / constraints
- Drifting into generic premium wellness minimalism.
- Over-decorating and losing single dominant read.
- Introducing too many style moves at once.

## Creative implications (non-visual)
- Every artifact needs one-sentence intent.
- Claims must be specific and verifiable where possible.
- Keep language compressed; avoid wellness cliches and abstract inspiration copy.
