# Creative Brief (Zara)

## Objective
Build an ownable Sol Vida system that feels restrained and premium while remaining explicit, useful, and scalable across packaging and digital surfaces.

## Brand adjectives (ranked)
1. Controlled
2. Clear
3. Warm
4. Modern
5. Calm

## What it must do
- Communicate flavor and benefit in under 3 seconds.
- Enforce one dominant read per surface.
- Prove series consistency across six outputs.
- Convert critique into durable rules (not one-off fixes).

## What it must NOT do
- No generic wellness wallpaper aesthetics.
- No decorative typography without hierarchy function.
- No multi-accent color noise.
- No claims without nearby evidence or concrete detail.

## Visual direction candidates (2-3)
### Direction A
- Name: Quiet Solar Utility
- Why it works: restrained structure + one warm charge creates clarity without coldness.
- Risks: can become too plain if contrast and scale are weak.
- Reference anchors: Kenya Hara, Vignelli, Rams.

### Direction B
- Name: Editorial Ingredient Grid
- Why it works: information-forward composition improves trust and differentiation.
- Risks: can feel clinical if warmth cues are underplayed.
- Reference anchors: Tufte, NYT information design, Purple editorial pacing.

### Direction C
- Name: Night Calm Premium
- Why it works: city-night tone aligns with modern premium wellness behavior.
- Risks: can drift into luxury cosplay if not tied to functional proof.
- Reference anchors: 032c restraint, modern magazine systems.

## Selected direction for round 1
Direction A: Quiet Solar Utility.

## System components to define
- Logo/mark: keep existing moon/gate geometry constraints.
- Typography: one display serif + one text sans, with hard usage constraints.
- Color/material: role-based palette (BG/text/accent/utility).
- Grid/layout: packaging + digital modular grid with fixed spacing scale.
- Motion/3D (if any): none required in round 1.
- Web components: hero, feature row, CTA block.
- Packaging system (if any): front label hierarchy + SKU variation rules.

## Approval gates
- Zara sign-off required for:
  - Direction
  - Typographic system
  - Final brand system
- Reject any round that violates One Move Rule or lacks 6-surface proof.
