# Rowan Brief — Iteration 02

## Round 1 Readout
- Mood direction is strong.
- Failure mode: hierarchy is too gentle.
- Required delta: increase brand prominence and reduce copy density.

## Audience Context
- Health-conscious adults (25-45) shopping for premium PM recovery supplements.
- They expect calm luxury cues plus clinical clarity.

## Positioning Delta for Round 2
- Shift from "soft editorial" to "calm authority":
  - Brand should read first and decisively.
  - Product/function should read second.
  - Facts + use/warnings should read quickly with no visual congestion.

## Hierarchy Priorities
1. Brand mark is the dominant read in left panel.
2. Product name and short descriptor stay secondary, concise.
3. Supplement facts remain dense but scannable in fixed center panel.
4. Right panel copy is compressed to avoid wrapping fatigue.

## Success Criteria
- Cyborg rails preserved: `1650x600`, `1fr 300px 1fr`, `<=8` ingredient rows.
- Label demonstrates one clear dominant read (brand first).
- Copy density reduced across label/board without losing trust signals.
- Product mock clearly displays upgraded hierarchy at distance.
- Brand board communicates the system, not just mood.
