# Sol Vida — Design Round 1 (notes)

## Front Label Layout System
- Top band: small “Sol Vida” wordmark centered or slightly left; keep clear space (foil/spot reserved for mark if used).
- Benefit badge: top-right circle or rounded pill (e.g., “Hydration + Glow”); ~15–20% label width; high contrast.
- Flavor focus block: mid-center hero flavor name in largest type (2–3 words); single line if possible.
- Low‑sugar claim: beneath flavor or under brand (e.g., “Low Sugar • 2g”); secondary pill/flag.
- Ingredient trio icons: bottom row, three line icons with labels (e.g., “Citrus • Turmeric • Sea Salt”), width ~70–80% of label.

## Color‑Block Strategy
- Two‑tone block: upper 60–65% lighter, lower 35–40% deeper tone.
- Flavor coding: dominant background per SKU; accent for badge + low‑sugar pill.
- Contrast: flavor sits on lightest area for legibility; ingredients on darker base.

## Type Hierarchy
- Brand: small caps or serif/sans mix; modest scale.
- Flavor: primary display font, largest size.
- Benefit badge: uppercase condensed or bold sans.
- Low‑sugar: smaller sans; uppercase + dot separators.
- Ingredients: simple sans, medium weight, 8–10pt relative scale.

## Spacing / Alignment
- Center‑aligned vertical stack; consistent margins.
- 6–8% padding all around.
- Consistent corner radius on pills/badges.

## Variants (if needed)
- Diagonal color split
- Vertical side band
- Angled badge
