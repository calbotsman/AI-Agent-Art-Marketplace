# Sol Vida — Rowan Strategist Brief (v3)

## Audience
- Primary: 25–45, urban/suburban, wellness‑curious but time‑scarce; values convenience, taste, and small “treat‑yourself” rituals.
- Secondary: 45–60, health‑motivated, looking to reduce sugar/alcohol; prefers trusted, transparent brands.
- Motivators: clean ingredients, functional benefits (energy, calm, digestion), low sugar, social shareability.
- Frictions: skepticism about “wellness hype,” price sensitivity, cluttered functional beverage shelf.

## Market Trends
- Functional beverages continue growth; “better‑for‑you” refreshment replacing soda/alcohol.
- Ingredient literacy rising: consumers scan for adaptogens, electrolytes, pre/probiotics, low/no sugar.
- Premiumization: people pay more for perceived efficacy + taste.
- Retail growth in specialty grocers, fitness studios, cafés; DTC subscription for loyalists.

## Social Behaviors
- “Micro‑rituals” (midday reset, post‑workout) drive repeat purchase.
- Social proof matters: creator endorsements, “what I drink” GRWM/desk setups.
- Visual cues used for quick health assessment (color, ingredient callouts, clean front‑of‑pack).
- Consumers share “brand values” alongside taste—sustainability, transparency, community.

## Competitive Context
- Crowded with functional seltzers/tonics; differentiation often via ingredient story or taste profile.
- Leaders own one clear promise (e.g., calm, focus, hydration) and repeat it consistently.
- Packaging trends: minimalism + bold functional claims; bright color blocking for shelf pop.

## Aesthetic Direction (reference only)
- Consider a “clean‑premium with approachable warmth” or “apothecary‑modern” cue set; emphasize clarity + benefit signals without clinical coldness.
