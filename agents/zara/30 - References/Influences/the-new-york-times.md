# The New York Times (NYT)

## 5-sentence summary
The NYT is an operating system for information: hierarchy, typography, and standards that must function at massive scale under deadline. Its best design lesson is controlled authority—calm structure that can absorb any topic, from breaking news to criticism. The Times also demonstrates how to evolve without breaking trust: incremental modernization, strong editorial values, and consistent voice. For brand builders, NYT is a reminder that credibility is *designed*—through consistency, clarity, and accountability mechanisms. For Zara, NYT is the reference for scalable editorial systems: templates, rules, and rigor that still leave room for wit.

## Steal this (8)
1. Design hierarchy first (headline, dek, nut graf, captions).
2. Build templates that survive chaos.
3. Use typography to signal authority and pace.
4. Treat corrections/standards as part of brand trust.
5. Create systems for varied media (text, photo, charts) to coexist.
6. Keep UI calm; let content spike when needed.
7. Design for accessibility and readability as non-negotiables.
8. Measure success by comprehension, not aesthetics.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **SaaS onboarding**: calm hierarchy and progressive disclosure.
- **Investor comms**: evidence-forward layouts that feel credible.
- **Marketplace apps**: complex information presented as navigable structure.
