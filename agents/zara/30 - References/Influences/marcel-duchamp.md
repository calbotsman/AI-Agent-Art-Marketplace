# Marcel Duchamp

## 5-sentence summary
Duchamp shifted the locus of art from making to choosing: the readymade is a context hack that turns selection into authorship. That’s essentially branding—framing, naming, placing, and declaring meaning around an object that might otherwise be ordinary. He also introduced a durable kind of humor: intellectual prank as critique of taste institutions. The modern risk is shallow imitation (“random object + irony”); the point is the *argument* about value, labor, and authority. For Zara, Duchamp is the masterclass in framing: the right naming, context, and rules can transform perception more than new visuals.

## Steal this (8)
1. Treat naming as design (titles change reality).
2. Change the frame before changing the object.
3. Use rules and gestures to create authorship (selection, editioning).
4. Build conceptual “moves” that can repeat across a system.
5. Use humor as a scalpel, not a vibe.
6. Make provenance visible (who chose this, why now?).
7. Attack assumptions about value (what counts as premium?).
8. Don’t hide behind irony—state the thesis.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Curation-led commerce** (Mr Porter, SSENSE): selection as identity.
- **Limited editions / drops**: framing + provenance + rules.
- **Productized “found” materials**: reclaimed/recontextualized goods with clear thesis.
