# Josef Albers

## 5-sentence summary
Albers is the scientist-poet of color: he shows that color is relational, not absolute. Through *Interaction of Color* and disciplined square paintings, he demonstrated how context changes perception—hue, value, edge vibration, and afterimage. His method is procedural: repeat a form, vary one variable, observe the effect. That makes him directly useful for brand systems, where consistency depends on controlled variation. For Zara, Albers is the playbook for palettes that feel intentional and for systems where repetition becomes sophistication.

## Steal this (8)
1. Treat palette as relationships (pairs, contrasts), not a list of swatches.
2. Use fixed forms to test variation (same layout, new colorways).
3. Exploit simultaneous contrast to make limited palettes feel rich.
4. Design for how colors behave on screens vs print vs materials.
5. Keep one “anchor neutral” and build tension around it.
6. Use edge behavior (hard vs soft) to change perceived color.
7. Build a color grammar: rules for pairing and hierarchy.
8. Document experiments like a lab notebook.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Google Material / design tokens**: systematic color roles and contrasts.
- **Fashion capsules**: repeat silhouettes, rotate color interactions.
- **Data viz**: palettes that encode meaning without lying.
