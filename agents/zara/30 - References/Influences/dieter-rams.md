# Dieter Rams

## 5-sentence summary
Rams is the patron saint of disciplined reduction: not minimalism as a look, but as an ethical stance against clutter and dishonesty. His “Ten Principles for Good Design” came out of real industrial constraints at Braun—mass production, durability, and user trust. The enduring value is that his rules are operational: useful, understandable, unobtrusive, thorough, environmentally considerate. Rams also shows that restraint needs craft; otherwise it’s just emptiness. For Zara, Rams is a hard standard: if you can’t explain why an element exists, it doesn’t.

## Steal this (8)
1. Treat subtraction as a core competency (cut until it hurts, then cut once more).
2. Build principle checklists into reviews (not just taste debates).
3. Make honesty visible (materials, capabilities, limitations).
4. Prefer longevity over novelty (design against trend churn).
5. Keep interface language consistent and learnable.
6. Use proportion and spacing as primary aesthetics.
7. Make sustainability structural (repairability, modularity), not marketing.
8. Let “obtrusive” be a failing state—measure it.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Apple**: direct lineage in hardware restraint and product language.
- **IKEA**: democratic function-first design at scale (when done well).
- **B2B SaaS**: “less but better” dashboards—defaults, hierarchy, fewer knobs.
