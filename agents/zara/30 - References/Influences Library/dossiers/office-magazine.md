# Office Magazine

## 5-sentence summary
Office is a fashion/creative culture magazine (print-born, digitally active) with an unorthodox, slightly subversive posture—youthful but not naïve. It’s good at portrait/editorial work that feels contemporary and social-native without collapsing into influencer mush. The tone is “in the scene”: close enough to feel real, styled enough to feel intentional. Office also shows how a media brand becomes a network (events, music, interviews) rather than only a publication. For Zara, Office is a reference for modern portrait/editorial taste that’s clean, current, and casually cool.

## Steal this (8)
1. Keep portrait lighting naturalistic but controlled.
2. Style as “real” with one elevated twist.
3. Use simple layouts; let images carry identity.
4. Make subjects feel like friends, not idols.
5. Publish across categories (fashion/music/culture) with one tone.
6. Maintain a consistent color temperature.
7. Build recurring columns/series.
8. Let social distribution influence cropping and pacing.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Beauty/fashion brands**: portrait series as ongoing brand content.
- **Creator communities**: editorial spotlights that feel intimate.
- **Retail media**: magazine-like content that supports commerce without screaming.

## Image / visual keywords (3)

## Visual signature (3-5 references)
- Glossy, high-contrast fashion editorials — Often featuring gritty, urban backdrops.
- Issue covers — Bold, centered portraits with a consistent, thick logotype.
- Artist collaborations — Limited edition prints and objects that bridge mag/gallery.
