# Anthony Goicolea

## 5-sentence summary
Goicolea’s staged imagery and mixed-media work lives in psychological surrealism: youth, identity, doubles, and scenes that feel like dreams with sharp edges. He often uses self-referential casting and constructed situations to explore desire, discomfort, and social roles. The aesthetic is glossy enough to pull you in, but uncanny enough to keep you uneasy. For brands, he’s a reference for “beautiful but wrong” storytelling—where the tension is the hook. For Zara, Goicolea is how to do surreal narrative without fantasy clichés: keep it grounded in human psychology.

## Steal this (8)
1. Make the image a psychological question, not an illustration.
2. Use repetition/doubles to signal identity and tension.
3. Keep staging precise; surreal works best when craft is clean.
4. Let color be seductive while content is unsettling.
5. Use props as symbols (not random weirdness).
6. Direct gaze and body language like cinema.
7. Avoid obvious “surreal” tropes; make it personal.
8. Build series: one image is odd, a sequence becomes a world.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Fashion campaigns**: uncanny scenes that differentiate without gore.
- **Entertainment key art**: psychological surrealism for thrillers/dramas.
- **Brand films/stills**: identity narratives (doubling, masks, roles).
