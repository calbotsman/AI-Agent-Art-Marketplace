# i-D

## 5-sentence summary
i-D is youth culture portraiture turned into a global fashion/editorial institution—street-level access with editorial authority. Its signature is the wink and the close-up: faces, identity, attitude, and a sense of “real people as style.” The magazine’s best lesson is how to build a recognizable editorial gesture (tone + composition + casting) that scales. i-D also shows how to stay culturally relevant by being present in scenes rather than commenting from above. For Zara, i-D is the reference for portrait-forward identity systems and casting-as-brand.

## Steal this (8)
1. Make casting a design decision.
2. Use portrait format as identity: consistent framing and gaze.
3. Keep typography secondary to people.
4. Build a tone of intimacy (not polish) when needed.
5. Elevate street sources without sanitizing them.
6. Let subcultures speak in their own visual language.
7. Treat interviews as product: voice builds trust.
8. Keep a recognizable “gesture” (the wink) as a repeatable code.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Beauty brands**: portrait-first campaigns that feel human.
- **Community platforms**: creator spotlights as core content.
- **Fashion startups**: casting and styling as the brand before budgets exist.
