# Michael Bierut

## 5-sentence summary
Bierut is the antidote to mystique: he explains design like it’s a job you can learn, not a gift you either have or don’t. At Pentagram and beyond, he’s known for identity systems that are typographically grounded, conceptually simple, and actually workable in the real world. His writing is as influential as his marks—case studies that make process legible without killing the romance. Bierut’s best lesson is that constraints (client reality, budgets, committees) are not enemies; they’re the medium. For Zara, Bierut is the system-builder influence: create rules people can follow and still feel like the brand.

## Steal this (8)
1. Write the strategy in plain language before drawing.
2. Pick one strong organizing idea and let everything obey it.
3. Design systems, not logos (variants, hierarchy, usage).
4. Treat typography as the primary identity engine.
5. Make it work at the worst touchpoint (forms, receipts, error states).
6. Show your work: process artifacts build trust and alignment.
7. Embrace iteration; good ideas survive versioning.
8. Optimize for adoption by non-designers.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Civic branding** (cities/transit): systems that survive scale and politics.
- **SaaS rebrands**: typographic identities + flexible components.
- **Media brands**: editorial templates that create consistency under deadline.
