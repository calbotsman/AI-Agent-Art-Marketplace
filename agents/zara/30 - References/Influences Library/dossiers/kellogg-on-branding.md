# *Kellogg on Branding* (Tybout / Calkins)

## 5-sentence summary
*Kellogg on Branding* is the MBA-grade operating manual: positioning, meaning, architecture, extensions, portfolios—the whole machine. It’s not sexy, but it’s the scaffolding that keeps “taste” from collapsing into vibes when money and growth show up. The book is most useful as a shared language for stakeholders: category definition, points of parity/difference, brand equity, and consistency vs adaptation. Its weakness is that it can produce safe, consensus brands if you treat it as a checklist rather than strategy. For Zara, it’s the complement to the art/design influences: the discipline to translate taste into decisions, boundaries, and metrics.

## Steal this (8)
1. Write positioning as a decision: category + audience + promise + reasons-to-believe.
2. Separate core equity from campaign expression.
3. Define points of parity before chasing points of difference.
4. Manage brand architecture intentionally (house, sub-brands, endorsed).
5. Treat extensions as equity trades—be explicit about what you spend.
6. Build internal governance: who approves, what rules, what exceptions.
7. Measure equity with leading indicators (consideration, trust), not just sales.
8. Keep a “consistency vs relevance” framework for new channels.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Scaling startups**: brand architecture and naming as product lines multiply.
- **Creator brands**: maintain core equity while expanding into new categories.
- **Retail portfolios**: clarify masterbrand vs line brands to reduce confusion.
