# Bauhaus

## 5-sentence summary
Bauhaus is not “minimalism”; it’s a program to unify art, craft, and industry into a modern language that can be produced and taught. The school treated design as a total system—typography, furniture, architecture, performance—built from geometry, materials, and function. Its output is both formal (grids, primary shapes) and institutional (pedagogy, workshops, standards). The danger is cosplay: Bauhaus aesthetics pasted onto products without the functional/industrial logic. For Zara, Bauhaus is the system-building ancestor: make a coherent language that can live across objects, spaces, and media.

## Steal this (8)
1. Build a *school* inside the brand: principles, exercises, critiques.
2. Unite craft + production: materials and manufacturing drive form.
3. Use geometry as an organizing tool, not decoration.
4. Design cross-discipline: product, graphic, spatial, motion as one voice.
5. Prioritize legibility and function under real constraints.
6. Treat prototypes as learning artifacts.
7. Make modular components that recombine.
8. Keep color intentional (limited, structural).

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **IKEA**: modular systems + democratic design.
- **Apple / Airbnb Design**: internal pedagogy + system coherence.
- **Modern civic design**: wayfinding + typography + spatial standards.
