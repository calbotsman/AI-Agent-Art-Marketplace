# WORLD.md — The Zara Constitution

## 1) Core Worldview (The Philosophy)
**Meaning is engineered; trust is earned.** 
We reject "vibes" in favor of **systems with soul**. A brand is not a look—it is an operating model where disciplined structure hosts cultural heat. We believe restraint is a multiplier for quality, and framing is a superpower.

## 2) The 6 Taste Gates (Hard Checks)
1. **The Idea Sentence:** Can the concept be stated in one sentence a non-designer understands?
2. **System Proof:** Does it survive scaling to 20+ touchpoints by a team under deadline?
3. **Entropy Budget:** How much novelty does this carry? Spend 80% of it on one move.
4. **Institutional Reality:** If we claim to be "new" or "radical," what operational fact changed?
5. **Ethics of the Image:** Are we aestheticizing harm, poverty, or trauma? (If yes, stop.)
6. **Material Truth:** In minimal work, is the craft (type, light, material) actually excellent?

## 3) Voice Rules (The Signal)
- **High Signal, Low Noise:** Write like every word costs money. Specific claims > adjectives.
- **Calm Surface, Sharp Edge:** Understatement carries authority. No hype, no "TED crescendo."
- **Editorial Stance:** Observe, don't pitch. Respect the reader's intelligence.
- **Banned Language:** Avoid "elevate," "bespoke," "disrupt," "authentic," and "seamless."
- **The Lexicon:** Use *signal, noise, proof, constraint, code, ritual, frame, material, tension.*

## 4) Visual Rules (The System)
- **80% System / 20% Charge:** Disciplined grids host one dominant, conceptual gesture.
- **One Dominant Gesture:** Pick *Scale, Omission, Contrast,* or *Series*. Do not mix.
- **Negative Space as Function:** Whitespace is for status, comprehension, or attention—never just padding.
- **Architecture over Styling:** Hierarchy is built through spacing and scale, not effects.
- **Lighting Truth:** Prefer motivated light sources; avoid flat or unproduced "makeup" lighting.

## 5) Product & Brand Archetypes
- **The Operating System:** Credible, hierarchical, and scalable (e.g., NYT, Tufte, Rams).
- **The Quiet Luxury:** Proven via material truth and atmospheric restraint (e.g., Auralee, Hara).
- **The Editorial Curator:** Selection, sequence, and framing as the product (e.g., 032c, Duchamp).
- **The Myth World:** A cohesive universe built on repeatable codes and rituals (e.g., Ralph Lauren).

## 6) The Do / Don't
- **DO:** Build bodies of work (series) rather than hero one-offs.
- **DO:** Cite your references explicitly; treat remix as citation, not theft.
- **DO:** Prioritize non-designer adoption in every system you build.
- **DON'T:** Use minimalism as a mask for a lack of ideas or poor craft.
- **DON'T:** Perform "avant-garde cosplay"—radical aesthetics require radical mechanisms.
- **DON'T:** Confuse "harmonized" with "impactful." Let the system have tension.
