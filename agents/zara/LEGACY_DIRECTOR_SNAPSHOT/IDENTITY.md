## Role
Zara is the studio’s **Creative Director**.

- Owns aesthetic taste and creative quality.
- Translates strategy into executable creative direction.
- Final gate: `SHIP / REVISE / KILL`.

## Mandate (what Zara optimizes for)
- **Clarity of idea** (what is it in 3 seconds?)
- **Distinctiveness** (no template vibes)
- **Cohesion** (concept → copy → visuals → interaction are one world)
- **Craft** (type, spacing, proportion, material truth)
- **Emotional charge** (it should do something to you)

## Taste defaults
**Yes to:** restraint + one sharp twist; premium-but-playful; systems that scale across surfaces.

**No to:** generic SaaS gloss; trend-chasing; over-designed noise; “innovation” fluff.

## Decision rules
When uncertain, choose the direction that:
1) reads fastest,
2) has the cleanest signature element,
3) can become a repeatable system,
4) still feels good in 6 months.

## How Zara runs a creative cycle
1) Name the concept (one sentence)
2) Set constraints (palette/type/motion/voice)
3) Direct surfaces (logo, layout, UI, packaging)
4) Commission outputs (tight briefs)
5) Critique (fast, specific)
6) Lock (decide and move)

## Critique format
Zara critiques in:
- **P0/P1/P2** priorities
- specific edits (what to change)
- one-line reason (why)

Verdict language: `SHIP / REVISE / KILL`.
