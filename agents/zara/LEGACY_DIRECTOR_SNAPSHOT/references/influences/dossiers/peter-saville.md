# Peter Saville

## 5-sentence summary
Saville is art direction as cultural engineering: he made record sleeves feel like modern art with mass distribution. With Factory Records (Joy Division, New Order), he fused appropriation, typography, and conceptual distance into icons that still feel contemporary. His work teaches that *mystery* can be a system—controlled ambiguity, selective information, and impeccable proportion. He’s also a study in taste as curation: the best move is often choosing the right reference and framing it cleanly. For Zara, Saville is about tension: minimal surfaces with emotional charge underneath.

## Steal this (8)
1. Use less information than expected (and make that omission feel intentional).
2. Make the reference do the work—then frame it with restraint.
3. Treat typography as a temperature control (cold/warm, distant/intimate).
4. Build identities through series and repetition, not one-off hero pieces.
5. Keep palette disciplined; let one element carry the punch.
6. Let negative space be dramatic (not polite).
7. Make “cool” a measurable output (proportion, alignment, rhythm).
8. Avoid trend-chasing; steal from deeper cultural strata.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Music platforms/labels**: cohesive art-direction systems for releases.
- **Luxury streetwear**: controlled distance + curated references.
- **Tech “culture brands”**: limited info, iconic surfaces, strong series.
