# Ralph Lauren

## 5-sentence summary
Ralph Lauren is lifestyle worldbuilding executed with ruthless consistency: products are props for a coherent American myth. The brand sells an edited history—prep, western, nautical, country club—assembled into a universe that feels timeless even when it’s invented. Its power comes from codes (equestrian, crest, stripe, denim, tweed) repeated across categories and decades. RL also shows how to do aspiration without futurism: the future is “better past.” For Zara, Ralph is the blueprint for building a brand world big enough to live inside.

## Steal this (8)
1. Create a world with rules (places, characters, rituals) and keep it stable.
2. Use heritage codes as modular motifs.
3. Make product photography like cinema stills from your universe.
4. Sell ensembles and lifestyles, not isolated SKUs.
5. Build seasonal narratives that still feel like the same book.
6. Repeat signature materials (oxford cloth, denim, wool) as identity.
7. Keep typography and marks classic; let styling do the variation.
8. Let retail/space reinforce the myth (materials, music, scent).

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Aimé Leon Dore**: neighborhood mythology + consistent codes.
- **J.Crew (at its best)**: edited Americana and ensembles.
- **Hospitality brands**: worldbuilding through interiors + uniforms + rituals.
