# Naoto Fukasawa

## 5-sentence summary
Fukasawa designs for the moment *before* you notice design—the “without thought” zone where behavior is automatic. His work (Muji, ±0, and broader product practice) is quiet but not blank: it’s empathetic, specific, and tuned to human habits. He’s famous for stripping away “features” until the object becomes an interface for a simple action. The sophistication is in what’s *not* there: reduced seams, softened geometry, and frictionless affordances. For Zara, Fukasawa is the north star for product and UX that disappears—but still feels inevitable and premium.

## Steal this (8)
1. Design from observed behavior, not surveys.
2. Aim for “unconscious usability”: the right thing happens naturally.
3. Reduce seams (visual + functional) until the object reads as one move.
4. Keep a single dominant gesture per product/page.
5. Make affordances gentle and obvious (no novelty tax).
6. Use neutrality to increase *adoption*, not to look tasteful.
7. Put craft where hands land (edges, weight, balance).
8. Let the context complete the form—don’t over-design.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Muji**: non-ego product language that scales across categories.
- **Apple hardware UX**: single-gesture dominance (wake, unlock, pay).
- **Modern fintech apps**: one-path flows (sign up, transfer, invest) with minimal branching.
