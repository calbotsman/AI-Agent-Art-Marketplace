# Kenya Hara

## 5-sentence summary
Kenya Hara is minimalism with *meaning*: not “less” as an aesthetic, but emptiness as a receptive field for perception and culture. As Muji’s art director and a prolific writer (notably *Designing Design*), he argues that design should sharpen awareness—materials, rituals, and everyday life—rather than shout. His work leverages whiteness, paper, and silence to create a high-resolution feeling of calm. Importantly, Hara is not generic modernism; he’s tuned to Japanese notions of void, interval, and implicit communication. For Zara, Hara is the playbook for restraint that still feels rich—where absence becomes a brand asset.

## Steal this (8)
1. Use “empty space” as a functional interface (breathing room = comprehension).
2. Make materials the story (paper, fabric, grain, light).
3. Design for attentiveness: slow the eye, reduce distraction.
4. Keep identity codes few but sacred (one typeface, one tone, one rule).
5. Prefer quiet hierarchy over loud contrast.
6. Let photography be descriptive, not aspirational.
7. Build systems that can host diversity without losing coherence.
8. Use whiteness/neutrality only when it carries intention (not trend).
