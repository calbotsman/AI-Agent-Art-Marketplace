# Annie Leibovitz

## 5-sentence summary
Leibovitz is the high-production portrait queen: celebrities staged as characters, photographed with cinematic lighting and meticulous art direction. Her work proves that portraiture can be both intimate and myth-making—human faces inside constructed narratives. The brand lesson is orchestration: wardrobe, set, gesture, and light all align to a single idea. She also demonstrates how editorial platforms (Rolling Stone, Vanity Fair, Vogue) shape visual culture over decades. For Zara, Leibovitz is the reference for iconic portrait campaigns that feel like cultural events.

## Steal this (8)
1. Direct subjects like actors; pose is storytelling.
2. Use production design to externalize personality.
3. Make lighting sculptural and consistent.
4. Keep the concept readable instantly.
5. Build a stable visual signature across varied subjects.
6. Prioritize expression over perfection.
7. Choose locations/sets that act as symbols.
8. Treat portraits as brand assets with long shelf life.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Luxury brand ambassadors**: portraits as mythology.
- **Streaming originals**: character posters with editorial craft.
- **Founder-led brands**: staged portraits that signal authority.
