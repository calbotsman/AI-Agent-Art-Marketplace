# Mies van der Rohe

## 5-sentence summary
Mies is structural clarity turned into atmosphere: the building is an exact system, and the experience is calm authority. “Less is more” is often misread as visual emptiness; for Mies it’s about reducing to essential structure, proportion, and material truth. The drama comes from precision—steel, glass, stone—and the choreography of grids and planes. He also teaches that minimalism is expensive: it forces quality because there’s nowhere to hide. For Zara, Mies is the guide for spatial/brand environments: make the structure obvious, the details perfect, and let the space do the persuasion.

## Steal this (8)
1. Treat grid/structure as the primary brand asset.
2. Use a limited material palette and execute it flawlessly.
3. Make details do the talking (junctions, seams, edges).
4. Build calm authority through proportion and rhythm.
5. Design emptiness as a feature (negative space with purpose).
6. Separate “structure” and “skin” (system vs expression).
7. Choreograph movement: what the user sees first/next/last.
8. If you go minimal, raise the quality bar across everything.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Flagship retail**: architectural calm that makes product feel inevitable.
- **Luxury web UX**: slow, spacious hierarchy with perfect type.
- **Packaging**: rigid structure + one premium material + minimal print.
