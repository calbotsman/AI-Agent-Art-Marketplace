# 032c

## 5-sentence summary
032c is a Berlin-born culture/fashion magazine that treats editorial as a curated thesis, not a feed. Its value is art direction that feels contemporary without being trend-chasing: clean typography, high-concept imagery, and writing that’s willing to be critical. 032c makes “culture” feel like a designed object—sequenced, paced, and opinionated. It also exemplifies how to build a brand around taste: the magazine is the product and the portfolio. For Zara, 032c is the model for an editorial voice that’s sharp, international, and self-aware.

## Steal this (8)
1. Treat each issue as a *world* with a governing idea.
2. Keep typography modern but neutral—let content carry heat.
3. Pair long-form thinking with bold image pacing.
4. Use art direction as a point of view, not decoration.
5. Maintain a consistent “temperature” across disparate subjects.
6. Make contributor curation part of the brand.
7. Design covers as signals (not just portraits).
8. Build brand extensions (events, product) that feel editorial, not merch.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Brand magazines**: issue-based releases instead of constant content.
- **Fashion e-comm editorial**: treat product as cultural artifact.
- **Creative studios**: published voice as business development.
