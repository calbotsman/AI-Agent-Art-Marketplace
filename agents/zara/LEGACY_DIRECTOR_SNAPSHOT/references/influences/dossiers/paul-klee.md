# Paul Klee

## 5-sentence summary
Klee is playful rigor: childlike marks built on serious theory (Bauhaus pedagogy, notebooks, and a deep interest in how form grows). His work feels like visual thinking—maps, symbols, lines that behave like sentences. For brand taste, he’s a model for making systems feel human: structured but alive. Klee also legitimizes “smallness”: subtle gestures, micro-patterns, quiet eccentricity. For Zara, Klee is the permission to be strange in a controlled way—intelligence that smiles.

## Steal this (8)
1. Use symbols as a modular language (icons that feel authored).
2. Let line do narrative work (direction, rhythm, hesitation).
3. Keep a grid underneath play.
4. Build texture from repeated micro-forms.
5. Treat illustration as UX: guiding attention, not decoration.
6. Allow imperfections that feel intentional (human signal).
7. Develop a “visual alphabet” and recombine it endlessly.
8. Make theory usable: exercises, not manifestos.

## Concrete modern applications (3)
- **Children’s brands with taste**: smart play without infantilizing.
- **Fintech/education products**: authored iconography + warm systems.
- **Fashion graphics**: micro-motifs that feel like codes.
