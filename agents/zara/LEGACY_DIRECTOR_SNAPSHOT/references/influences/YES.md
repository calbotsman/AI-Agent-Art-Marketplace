# Influences — YES (tight steals + citations)

> Seed list from Josh (2026-02-16). This file is the “actionable” layer; deeper notes live in `dossiers/`.

## Philosophy / Criticism / Theory
- **Max Bense** — *taste as a system* (entropy/redundancy/signal). Steal: formalize “vibe” into rules you can teach + test.  
  Refs: https://www.hfg-ulm.info/en/department-head_max-bense.html
- **Carl Jung** — *archetypes + shadow* for coherent myth and transformation. Steal: define the brand’s role + ritual; name what you refuse to be.  
  Refs: https://www.junginstitute.ch/en/
- **Susan Sontag** — *form + ethics*; cut interpretive sludge. Steal: experience-first work; no aestheticizing harm for cool.  
  Refs: https://www.nybooks.com/contributors/susan-sontag/
- **Peter Bürger — Theory of the Avant-Garde** — *anti-cosplay filter*. Steal: if you borrow “subversion,” change an institution/operation (authorship, access, participation).  
  Refs: https://www.upress.umn.edu/book-division/books/theory-of-the-avant-garde

## Graphic / Brand / Industrial Design
- **Tibor Kalman** — editorial design that takes a side. Steal: concept-first layouts that read like arguments; typography as voice.  
  Refs: https://www.aiga.org/medalist-tibor-kalman
- **Naoto Fukasawa** — “without thought” usability. Steal: one dominant gesture; remove seams until use is automatic.  
  Refs: https://www.naotofukasawa.com/
- **Kenya Hara** — emptiness as a feature. Steal: restraint with material meaning; whitespace as interface.  
  Refs: https://www.lars-muller-publishers.com/designing-design
- **Dieter Rams** — principle-led reduction (ethics, not style). Steal: subtraction + honesty + longevity; build principle checklists into reviews.  
  Refs: https://www.vitsoe.com/us/about/dieter-rams
- **Michael Bierut** — systems that ship. Steal: plain-language strategy → typographic identity → templates that non-designers can use.  
  Refs: https://www.pentagram.com/
- **Massimo Vignelli** — grid discipline + limited toolset. Steal: fewer choices, higher-quality decisions; manuals that enforce coherence.  
  Refs: https://www.vignelli.com/
- **Scott Stowell / Open** — playful intelligence. Steal: one unexpected twist per piece; constraints as idea engines.  
  Refs: https://notclosed.com/
- **Peter Saville** — cool framing + controlled ambiguity. Steal: omission as a system; references framed with perfect proportion.  
  Refs: https://www.petersaville.com/
- **Bauhaus** — modern design as pedagogy + production. Steal: internal “school” (principles/exercises) + cross-discipline coherence.  
  Refs: https://www.bauhaus-dessau.de/en/

## Architecture
- **Mies van der Rohe** — structure-first calm authority. Steal: minimal surfaces demand perfect detail; choreograph space like UX.  
  Refs: https://www.miesvanderrohe-stiftung.com/

## Fine Art / Conceptual Art
- **James Turrell** — light as material. Steal: design attention and time; atmosphere as brand asset.
  Refs: https://jamesturrell.com/
- **Josef Albers** — color as relationship. Steal: fixed form + controlled variation; palette grammar, not swatch lists.  
  Refs: https://www.albersfoundation.org/
- **Henri Matisse** — simplification with joy. Steal: bold flat color + confident contour; cut-out thinking (big pieces).  
  Refs: https://www.tate.org.uk/art/artists/henri-matisse-1563
- **Pablo Picasso** — concept survives formal reinvention. Steal: aggressive iteration; reassemble form to reveal essentials.  
  Refs: https://www.museepicassoparis.fr/en
- **Paul Klee** — playful systems. Steal: visual alphabet + grid-under-play; authored symbols that recombine.  
  Refs: https://www.zpk.org/en/collection
- **Marcel Duchamp** — framing as authorship. Steal: naming/context/provenance can transform value more than new visuals.  
  Refs: https://www.philamuseum.org/collections/artist/duchamp-marcel

## Culture / Fashion
- **Virgil Abloh** — remix + distribution. Steal: citations as content; modular motifs across categories; build a platform that hosts culture.  
  Refs: https://www.virgilabloh.com/

## Fashion / Heritage Brands
- **Ralph Lauren** — lifestyle worldbuilding. Steal: cohesive universe + repeatable codes; sell roles/rituals, not items.  
  Refs: https://www.ralphlauren.com/
- **A.P.C.** — understatement as identity. Steal: cut/material as signature; quiet typography; repeat purchase rhythm.  
  Refs: https://www.apc.fr/
- **Auralee** — texture-forward minimal luxury. Steal: fabric proof + atmospheric color; minimal silhouettes to showcase drape.  
  Refs: https://auralee.jp/
- **Yves Saint Laurent (YSL)** — iconic codes + black discipline. Steal: few codes, repeated; sharp typography; elegant aggression.  
  Refs: https://museeyslparis.com/en

## Information Design
- **Edward Tufte** — high signal-to-noise. Steal: dense truth; small multiples; annotate claims with context.  
  Refs: https://www.edwardtufte.com/tufte/

## Business / Brand Strategy
- **Kellogg on Branding (Tybout / Calkins)** — the scaffolding. Steal: positioning + architecture + governance so taste can scale without dilution.  
  Refs: https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Kellogg+on+Branding+in+a+Hyper+Connected+World-p-9781119533184

  Refs: https://i-d.co/
- **The New York Times** — hierarchy at scale. Steal: template rigor + trust mechanics; calm authority under deadline.  
  Refs: https://www.nytimes.com/

  Refs: https://purple.fr/

  Refs: https://www.papermag.com/

  Refs: https://ourculturemag.com/
- **Office Magazine** — contemporary portrait/editorial network. Steal: social-native pacing with clean restraint; series-based publishing.  
  Refs: https://officemagazine.net/

  Refs: https://www.gagosian.com/artists/gregory-crewdson/

  Refs: https://www.davidhilliard.com/
